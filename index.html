Avanti's Pizzeria

Medina, N.Y.

2005-2008

Avanti's was my first job in high school. Even though I had worked on and off for my family's farm for years,
this was my first taste of real employment. I learned the in's and out's of restaurant service work and an
introduction to the joys of the customer service industry. Little did I know this job wold lay the groundwork for
my current career. My time there ended when I started college, though i still visited when I was home from school.

Pioneer Credit Recovery

Perry N.Y.

2012

After I had left college without my degree I was in need of a job. My hometown was small so prospects were slim.
A debt collection job nearby paid well and seemed like an interesting change of pace. I quickly learned that
debt collection was not the job for me. I was collectiong on the same loans that I had used for college.
I couldnt force myself to hound people about their debts. I heard so many stories of people that were down
on their luck and couldnt pay their debts.But I have always had a good work ethic, so I stuck with it.
After 10 months I decided to try moving west.

Safeway

Seattle W.A.

2012

I started at Safeway three days after I arrived in Seattle. This was my first interaction with west coast chains.
Top's Friendly Market ® was now Safeway ®. Tim Hortons ® was now Starbucks ®. I had never seen a supermarket that
sold liquor and wine. In New York, wine and liquor can only be sold at liquor stores. And they are closed on sundays.
I had never seen someone bring their dog into a supermarket before either. But I learned alot about my new home,
its people, and the differences of the coasts.

The Ballard Landmark

Seattle W.A.

2012-2013

The next thing I learned about Seattle is that all the nice things come at a cost, that cost being a 300% increase
in rent. So i needed a second, and possibly a third, source of income. I figured I'd use my high school restaurant
experience and try retirement homes. The job itself I didnt really care for, But the people made it all worth it.
I built friendships with some of the residents that I still keep with to this day. Sadly some have passed, but the
relationships I made with those residents will stay with me forever.

Ristorante Picolinos

Seattle W.A.

2012-

Ristorante Picolinos became that third job I was talking about. I learned that Seattle had a more favorable stance
towards the service industry. I applied at every restaurant I could find and ended up choosing Ristorante Picolinos.
I started working there two days a week as a bus boy. Over the years I learned about every aspect of a restaurant.
Iin the span of almost 5 years I have worked my way up to Restaurant Manager, studying Italian wines and foods, learning
the bookkeeping and the labor costs. I have built countless connections with clients in the restaurant, and even brought
some of my own in, from relationships with previous jobs. After a year of management I've learned alot about people,
food and drinks. I never would have thought that my job in high school would lead me to this point.
